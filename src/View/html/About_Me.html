My History
I grew up in a town so small even the people who drive through it don't know its name. The town's name is Bluff Dale, Texas. When I was 11, I moved to another small pass-through town named Hico, Texas. During this transition, I was able to skip 6th grade. Throughout high school, I excelled in computer science, going as far as to place 3rd in a state competition. Despite this, I still decided to major in premed at Tarleton State University. In my premed major, I was able to make A's, but it was not something that I loved. During my second year of college, I changed majors to software engineering and rediscovered my passion for the subject. I could not get enough of it. Tarleton's course went too slow for me, so I decided to look for accelerated programs; this led me to Western Governors University. I was so excited to start an accelerated program that I switched to WGU mid-way through the second semester of my new major. With only 18 credits towards my degree, I graduated from WGU with a bachelor's in software engineering in just six months. Now, I am a college graduate at 19 years old and ecstatic to find out what happens next!
